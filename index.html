Theoretical background

01Corner speed limit

At each point the car corners only as fast as lateral grip allows:

v² · |κ| = ay,max(v)

Corner ceiling:

vcorner = √( μg / (|κ| − kL) )
κ curvature · kL = μ·½ρCLA / m

02Grip with downforce

The lateral limit:

ay,max(v) = μg + kL·v²

Load sensitivity (the toggle): real tyres lose grip coefficient as load rises:

μ(Fz) = μ₀·[1 − kload·(Fz/Fz0 − 1)]

03Friction ellipse

Shared between cornering and braking/accelerating:

(ax/ay,max)² + (ay/ay,max)² ≤ 1

04Forward–backward solver

Two passes from the corner ceiling find the fastest legal speed everywhere via the energy equation:

vi+1² = vi² + 2·ax·Δs

Forward = acceleration-limited; backward = braking-limited. Lap time = Σ 2Δs/(vi+vi+1).
